About Me

Hi, I'm Parth Natekar!
I recently completed a Dual Degree (Bachelor's + Master's) in the Department of Engineering Design at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras. My research revolves around addressing broader issues around Deep Learning, like interpretability, privacy-preservation, uncertainty and robustness, and generalization, as well as Wearable Devices, Human-Computer Interaction, Biomedical Design, and Medical Imaging. I'm specifically interested in applications in the healthcare domain. Please take a look at my research/project portfolio. See more on my vision below.
GO to research portfolio
My other hobbies include playing football (I'm a Liverpool fan), painting (See the Art Section), and working out.

My Vision


​Broadly, my research vision has three aspects: (1) Building interpretable, reliable, and trustworthy Artificial Intelligence, (2) Improving interaction between humans and the upcoming digital environment around us through wearable devices and interactive modalities, and (3) Human-Centered Design to build devices and machines that can solve problems in sectors like healthcare, like diagnostic imaging or point-of-care sensing.
​
The pictogram on the right concisely summarizes this vision - building an intuitive, trustworthy environment through engineering and science, keeping human beings at the center. My research portfolio and publications show how I have been working towards this vision. Ideas I have for potential future projects are available on the future directions page. Please take a look at these if you are interested in collaborating.
